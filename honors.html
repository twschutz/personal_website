About Me

My name is Ted Schutz and I am a current Junior from Chicago pursuing my Bachelors of Science in Computer Science in Engineering with a minor in Statistics. I hope that this combination of my studies will allow me to learn more about and thrive in the field of Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence. I have been afforded some amazing experiences during my time at Michigan which include being a part of a research lab on campus, interning at a local law firm, as well as being a member of some amazing clubs! Read more below to learn more about my experiences and journey as an Engineering Honors student here at Michigan!

Experiences

Apex Trading Group

Apex Trading Group is one of the University of Michigan's Premier Trading Groups. As a member of ATG and the Technology Sector Pitch Team, I research macroeconomic trends, stock market news, company financials, and new technological developments to assess investment opportunities in the technology sector. I help analyze and constructively critique my teammates recommendations in order to choose a final company to pitch to the entire Apex Trading Group team. Our past pitches have exceeded the benchmark growth rates set by ATG leaders and have seen an average return of 18% in the past year alone. I hope to be able to eventually apply my knowledge of Computer Science and Finance to develop algorithmic trading strategies which utilize various technical indicators and company statistics in order to support stock pitches and identify areas for new investment opportunities.

MSAIL

MSAIL, the Michigan Student Artificial Intelligence Lab is, as the name suggests, a group of students who have a passion for exploring the field of Machine Learning and share their knowledge through weekly paper reviews, education meetings, and hands-on team projects that allow me to not only learn about recent developments in AI/ML but also to use this knowledge in a team-environment.

As a member of the Outreach Committee, I led a team of 15 teens to devise new ways to recruit teenagers from across Chicago to help those that are less fortunate than them. As a volunteer for over 400 hours, I drastically improved my communication and leadership skills and demonstrated my global and cultural awareness.
